## Tone and Voice
- Professional yet approachable
- Direct address to reader/patient
- Educational focus but never condescending
- Empathetic understanding of patient concerns
- Mix of urgency (health matters) with reassurance

## Content Approach
- Lead with hooks that engage the reader
- Organize information clearly with scannable structure
- Use real examples when possible
- Admit limitations and recommend professional consultation
- Include CTAs that invite action (book evaluation, learn more)

## Spanish-Specific Conventions
- Grammar: Formal "usted" with patient-friendly tone
- No accents/tildes in markdown body (encoding safety)
- Use hyphens instead of tildes where needed: "contáctanos" → "contactanos"
- Maintain professional medical vocabulary

## English-Specific Conventions
- American English spelling (color not colour, etc.)
- Direct, concise sentences
- Professional medical terminology
- Encourage action naturally

## SEO Integration
- Primary keyword in first 100 words naturally
- Secondary keywords in H2 and H3 headings
- Internal links contextually relevant, not forced
- External links to authoritative sources: AAD, Mayo Clinic, Skin Cancer Foundation, PubMed
- Meta descriptions must be compelling and include primary keyword

## Structural Elements
- Always include H1 (appears once, matches blog title concept)
- Use H2 for major sections
- Use H3 for subsections under H2s
- FAQ section for common reader questions
- Conclusion that reinforces value and CTAs
