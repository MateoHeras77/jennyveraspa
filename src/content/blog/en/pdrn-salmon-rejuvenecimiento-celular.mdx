PDRN (Polydeoxyribonucleotide) is one of the most interesting treatments for those seeking deep regeneration without invasiveness. Unlike hyaluronic acid, which hydrates superficially, salmon PDRN works at the cellular level to stimulate your skin to repair and rejuvenate itself.

At Jenny Vera Spa, we use it as part of advanced regenerative medicine protocols. Many patients have seen remarkable results combining it with [exosomes](/blog/exosomas-rejuvenecimiento) or [microneedling](/blog/microneedling-dermapen).

## What is PDRN and where does it come from

PDRN stands for Polydeoxyribonucleotide. It sounds complex, but simply explained: it's a molecule derived from salmon DNA that, when injected into the skin, sends biological signals to your cells to stimulate repair and regeneration.

### The scientific origin

PDRN is isolated from salmon reproductive glands. Scientists discovered that when processed in a special way, this molecule has extraordinary regenerative properties. After decades of research, it's now a validated dermatological treatment.

It's important to clarify: it's not "natural" in a romantic sense. It's a biologically active, purified, and standardized molecule that has passed rigorous pharmaceutical quality controls.

## How PDRN works in your skin

The mechanism is fascinating from a biology perspective:

### 1. Cellular receptor activation

PDRN binds to specific receptors (A2a adenosine receptors) on skin cell surfaces. This binding is like "unlocking a door" that was closed.

### 2. Repair chain initiated

Once these receptors are activated, the cell receives a message: "it's time to repair yourself." A process called neoangiogenesis (formation of new blood vessels) and neofibrogenesis (new collagen production) begins.

### 3. Healing and regeneration

Unlike permanent fillers, PDRN instructs your body to heal itself. It's biological therapy, not a filler.

### 4. Improved durability

Because it stimulates actual tissue regeneration, results tend to consolidate and last longer than topical hydrators or injected hyaluronic acid.

## Difference between PDRN and hyaluronic acid

Many patients confuse these treatments. Here are the key differences:

### PDRN
- Stimulates regeneration of your own tissue
- Cumulative effect (improves with sessions)
- Ideal for comprehensive rejuvenation
- Visible results in 2-4 weeks, improve until 3 months
- Needs 3-4 sessions for complete protocol

### Hyaluronic acid
- Hydrates and fills
- Immediate but temporary effect
- Ideal for volumizing specific areas
- Results visible instantly, last 6-12 months
- Visible "cushion" effect

The truth is many patients benefit from both. PDRN regenerates "aged" skin and hyaluronic acid provides immediate volume. At Jenny Vera Spa, we frequently design combined protocols.

## Indications: who benefits most

PDRN works especially well in these cases:

- **Thin, dull, aged skin** that needs regeneration, not just filling
- **Irregular texture** from sun damage or chronological aging
- **Fine lines** that don't respond well to topical hydration
- **Facial fatigue** from lack of luminosity and elasticity
- **Acne scars** (especially less deep ones)
- **Loss of tone and elasticity** in neck and décolletage
- **Post-procedure recovery** (accelerates healing after [microneedling](/blog/microneedling-dermapen) or peeling)
- **Sensitive skin** that needs deep repair without irritation

It's not ideal for those seeking immediate volumization; hyaluronic acid is better for that.

## PDRN treatment protocol

### Phase 1: Initial (intensive)

- **3 to 4 sessions** spaced 7-10 days apart
- Dose of 2-3ml per session
- Administered via mesotherapy or intradermal injection

### Phase 2: Consolidation

- **1 additional session** 1 month after completing initial phase
- Helps consolidate results

### Phase 3: Maintenance (optional)

- 1 session every 6-12 months depending on how your skin ages

Light inflammation or redness may occur in the first two weeks post-session, which easily subsides.

## Expected results

The typical progression is:

- **Days 1-3:** Light swelling (normal, disappears)
- **Week 1:** Skin feels fresher, better texture
- **Weeks 2-3:** Visible improvement in luminosity and tone
- **Week 4:** Reduction of fine lines, firmer skin
- **Weeks 6-12:** Consolidated result, maximum regeneration

Changes are gradual but consistent. It's not "dramatic before and after" like surgery, but it's real transformation.

## Combinations with other treatments

PDRN is enhanced when combined with:

### PDRN + Exosomes
This is a very effective combination. Exosomes provide multiple growth factors, and PDRN activates specific receptors. Together they create a powerful synergistic effect.

### PDRN + Microneedling
[Microneedling](/blog/microneedling-dermapen) opens "doors" in the skin, and PDRN enters through these microlesions to stimulate deep regeneration. Results are much better than each alone.

### PDRN + Mesotherapy
Both are administered via intradermal route. Many protocols combine them in the same session for efficiency.

## Recovery and care

Recovery is straightforward:

- **Days 1-3:** Mild swelling and redness may occur. This is normal
- **Week 1:** Avoid intense sun, heavy exercise, and sauna
- **Always:** Daily sun protection (SPF 50+)
- **Hydration:** Keep skin well hydrated; PDRN works better in hydrated skin

No downtime required. You can return to normal activities immediately.

## Realistic expectations

It's important to be honest about what PDRN can and cannot do:

### PDRN does
- Improve texture, tone, and luminosity
- Stimulate collagen and elastin
- Reduce moderate fine lines
- Regenerate damaged tissue
- Work well for superficial scars

### PDRN doesn't
- Eliminate deep wrinkles (those may need [HIFU](/blog/hifu-lifting-sin-cirugia) or surgery)
- Provide instant volumization (hyaluronic acid is better for that)
- Drastically change facial shape
