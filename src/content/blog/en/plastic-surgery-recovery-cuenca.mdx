## Why Recovery Care Matters as Much as the Surgery

A great surgery can still end with a disappointing result if the recovery is neglected. After procedures like liposuction, abdominoplasty (tummy tuck), body contouring, or a facelift, your body retains fluid and starts a normal inflammatory response. Left unmanaged, that process can slow you down and create real problems:

- **Swelling and heaviness** that make it hard to see your true result for weeks.
- **Fibrosis**, hardened lumps or uneven areas of scar tissue that form when fluid stagnates under the skin.
- **Seromas**, pockets of fluid that collect in the treated area and sometimes need to be drained by your surgeon.

None of this means something went wrong. It simply means the body needs help clearing what surgery leaves behind. That is exactly what post-op lymphatic drainage is designed to do.

## What Lymphatic Drainage Massage Actually Does

[Post-operative lymphatic drainage](/en/servicios/drenaje-postoperatorio) is a gentle, rhythmic massage technique that stimulates the lymphatic system, the network your body uses to move fluid and waste out of the tissues. It is nothing like a deep-tissue massage. The pressure is light and follows the natural direction of lymph flow.

The goal is straightforward: move the fluid that has built up after surgery so it drains away instead of settling. Done consistently, it helps to:

- Reduce swelling and the feeling of tightness.
- Prevent and soften fibrosis and hardened areas.
- Improve circulation and tissue oxygenation.
- Relieve discomfort and speed up recovery.
- Protect and optimize the final aesthetic result of your surgery.

## The Typical Protocol: Frequency and When to Start

Every surgeon and every procedure is different, so the protocol is always defined **following your surgeon's indications**. That said, here is what recovery usually looks like.

Sessions often begin within the first days after surgery, once your surgeon gives the go-ahead. Starting at the right moment matters, and that timing is a medical decision, not something to rush. Many protocols run between ten and twenty sessions in total, closer together at first, when your body needs the most help, and gradually spaced out as you improve.

## Red Flags of Poor Post-Op Care

Not all recovery care is equal, and choosing the wrong provider can undo good surgery. Be cautious if you encounter:

- **Aggressive, painful "massage."** Post-op drainage should be gentle. Hard pressure on healing tissue can cause harm.
- **No interest in your surgeon's instructions.** A serious provider asks about your procedure and coordinates with your medical team.
- **A one-size-fits-all package** with no initial evaluation or adjustment to your progress.
- **No hygiene or professional setting.** Recovery care should happen in a clean, appropriate space.

Trust your instincts. Your body is healing, and it deserves careful, professional hands.
