Aesthetic surgery doesn't end when you leave the operating room. The post-operative stage is crucial to ensure not only your safety but also the final quality of your results. At **Jenny Vera Spa** in Cuenca, we offer a comprehensive post-operative care protocol designed for each type of procedure.

## What Are Post-Operative Treatments?

Post-operative treatments comprise a set of clinical interventions designed to:

- Minimize inflammation and edema.
- Facilitate proper healing.
- Prevent complications.
- Accelerate return to normal activities.
- Optimize the appearance of your final result.

It's not a passive phase of "waiting to heal." It's an active stage where professional management makes a real difference.

## Recovery Protocols by Procedure Type

### Facial Procedures

After facial treatments like HIFU, deep peels, or injectables:

- **First 24 hours**: Avoid hot water, makeup, and direct sun exposure.
- **Days 2-7**: Gentle lymphatic drainage to reduce facial swelling.
- **Week 2 onward**: Deep hydration and mandatory sun protection.
- **Visible results**: Between 1 to 3 months depending on the treatment.

### Body Procedures

For laser hair removal, carboxyotherapy, intimate HIFU, or body treatments:

- **First week**: Regulated compression and specialized lymphatic drainage.
- **Weeks 2-4**: Progressive reactivation of the area as indicated.
- **Month 2 onward**: Result consolidation with care routines.
- **Precaution**: Avoid friction, extreme heat, and tight clothing for the first few weeks.

## Key Components of the Post-Operative Protocol

### 1. Initial Clinical Assessment

A specialized review within the first 48 hours:

- Vital signs and general condition monitoring.
- Inspection of the treated area.
- Evaluation of any unexpected reactions.
- Adjustments to the recovery plan based on progress.

### 2. Therapeutic Lymphatic Drainage

A gentle yet highly effective manual technique:

- Mobilizes stagnant fluids.
- Reduces swelling naturally.
- Improves tissue oxygenation.
- Accelerates healing.

**Recommended frequency**: Every 2-3 days for the first 2-3 weeks, then as needed.

### 3. Indicated Medications and Supplements

Depending on the procedure type:

- Anti-inflammatory medications (if indicated).
- Natural drainage agents to reduce fluid retention.
- Vitamins and minerals that enhance healing.
- Always under medical supervision.

### 4. Local Care of the Area

Specialized cleansings and treatments:

- Regenerative serums adapted to your skin type.
- Specialized bandages or dressings if necessary.
- Avoid self-medication on the area.

## Typical Recovery Timeline

**Week 1**
- Maximum swelling (natural and expected)
- Medical check-ups and drainage sessions
- Relative rest

**Weeks 2-3**
- Initial swelling decreases 50-70%
- Progressive reactivation
- Possibility of returning to light activities

**Month 2**
- Significant improvement in inflammation
- Appearance closer to the final result
- Safe start of gentle physical activity

**Month 3 onward**
- Result consolidation
- Stable and definitive appearance
- Return to normal routine

## Warning Signs: When to Contact Immediately

Although complications are rare, notify your doctor if:

- Fever above 38.5°C.
- Intense pain that's uncontrollable.
- Abnormal redness, warmth, or discharge.
- Severe burning or irritation.
- Asymmetrical or disproportionate swelling.
- Any unexpected changes.

Early detection of any anomaly is key to preserving your safety and results.

## Recommendations to Enhance Your Recovery

- **Hydration**: Drink at least 2-3 liters of water daily.
- **Proper nutrition**: Proteins, vitamin C, zinc, and iron promote healing.
- **Quality sleep**: Rest 7-8 hours, with elevated pillow if it's a facial procedure.
- **Avoid tobacco and alcohol**: They affect healing.
- **Sun protection**: SPF 50+ mandatory for at least 3 months.
- **No intense exercise**: Wait for your doctor's clearance.
