# Jenny Vera Spa — SEO and Marketing Action Plan

**Business:** Jenny Vera Spa, Advanced Aesthetics Center
**Location:** Edificio Plaza Médica, 4to piso, Av. Manuel de J. Calle, Cuenca, Ecuador
**Website:** https://www.jennyveraspa.com (bilingual: /es and /en)
**Goal:** Increase qualified organic traffic and bookings for a new domain in a competitive, geographically ambiguous market.

---

## How to use this document

Tasks are grouped into three phases by leverage and timeline.

- **Phase 1 — Quick wins:** high impact, low effort, do this week.
- **Phase 2 — Structural:** the foundation that makes everything else rank, do this month.
- **Phase 3 — Ongoing:** compounding work that builds authority over 3 to 6 months.

Each task has: what it is, why it matters, step-by-step actions, effort, and an acceptance criterion (how you know it is done). A progress tracker sits at the end.

**Core principle:** The website is good. The problem is visibility and authority, not the site. Win local intent in Cuenca, Ecuador. Do not chase national or international rankings.

## Phase 1 — Quick wins (this week)

### Task 1.1 — Fix the broken social media links

**What:** The footer Instagram and Facebook links currently point to the generic instagram.com and facebook.com homepages, not to the real business profiles.

**Why it matters:** Broken links kill social referral traffic, send visitors to a dead end, and weaken the brand-consistency signals Google uses to trust a local business.

**Steps:**
1. Locate the real Instagram and Facebook profile URLs for Jenny Vera Spa.
2. Update the footer links on every page template (the footer is shared, so one fix should propagate).
3. Verify the links open the actual profiles on both /es and /en.
4. While there, confirm the WhatsApp link works and pre-fills the correct number (+593 999 152 853).

**Effort:** 30 minutes.

**Done when:** Clicking the footer icons opens the real Instagram and Facebook profiles on both language versions.

---

### Task 1.2 — Optimize the Google Business Profile (highest local lever)

**What:** Fully build out and optimize the Google Business Profile (GBP). The profile already exists (the review link g.page/r/Cbso4rpRXTOYEAI is live).

**Why it matters:** For a local service business, GBP drives the map pack and "near me" results more than the website itself. This is the single highest-impact action on the list and it can move results in 30 to 60 days, faster than classic SEO.

**Steps:**
1. Claim and verify ownership of the profile if not already done.
2. Set the primary category correctly (for example "Medical spa" or "Skin care clinic"). Add relevant secondary categories (Spa, Facial spa, Laser hair removal service).
3. Complete every field: full address, phone, website, hours, and the appointment link pointing to /es/contacto.
4. Write a keyword-rich business description mentioning Cuenca, Ecuador and the core treatments (faciales, depilación láser, HIFU, drenaje postoperatorio, exosomas).
5. Add the full services list with short descriptions and prices where possible.
6. Upload high-quality photos: exterior, interior, equipment, team, and before and after where allowed. Aim for 15 or more.
7. Turn on messaging if the team can respond quickly.

**Effort:** 2 to 3 hours.

**Done when:** Profile is verified, all fields complete, categories set, and 15 or more photos uploaded.

---

### Task 1.3 — Launch a Google review engine

**What:** Build a simple, repeatable system to collect Google reviews from happy clients.

**Why it matters:** Review count and recency are among the strongest local ranking factors. New competitors with no website still beat polished sites when they have more reviews. This is how a new domain wins the map pack.

**Steps:**
1. Turn the review link (g.page/r/Cbso4rpRXTOYEAI/review) into a QR code.
2. Print the QR code on a small card at reception and add it to the WhatsApp post-appointment message.
3. Train staff to ask every satisfied client to leave a review before they leave.
4. Reply to every review, positive or negative, within 48 hours. Replies are a ranking and trust signal.
5. Target a steady pace, for example 4 to 8 new reviews per month, rather than a one-time burst.

**Effort:** 1 hour setup, then ongoing.

**Done when:** QR code is in use at reception and in WhatsApp, and a review-request step is part of the client checkout routine.

---

### Task 1.4 — Set up Google Search Console and confirm indexing

**What:** Connect the site to Google Search Console (GSC) and verify both /es and /en are indexed.

**Why it matters:** Without GSC you are blind. It shows what Google has indexed, what queries you appear for, and any crawl errors. It is step zero for measuring everything else.

**Steps:**
1. Add the property in Google Search Console and verify ownership (DNS or HTML tag).
2. Submit the sitemap (typically /sitemap.xml). Confirm it lists both language versions.
3. Use the URL Inspection tool on the homepage, /es/servicios, and /en equivalents to confirm they are indexed. Request indexing for any that are not.
4. Connect Google Analytics 4 if not already running, so you can track traffic and conversions.

**Effort:** 1 to 2 hours.

**Done when:** Property is verified, sitemap submitted, and the key pages show as indexed in GSC.

## Phase 2 — Structural (this month)

### Task 2.1 — Split treatments into individual indexable pages

**What:** Today all treatments live as anchor sections under one URL (/es/servicios#faciales, #corporales, and so on). Google sees this as one page. Create a dedicated, indexable page for each major treatment.

**Why it matters:** This is the highest-leverage SEO move on the list. One page can rank for one primary keyword. Eight treatment pages can rank for eight commercial keywords. Right now eight treatments are forced to share the authority of a single URL.

**Priority pages to create (each targets one keyword):**

| New page | Primary keyword | Search intent |
|---|---|---|
| /es/servicios/depilacion-laser | depilación láser Cuenca | Ready to book |
| /es/servicios/limpieza-facial | limpieza facial Cuenca | Ready to book |
| /es/servicios/hifu | HIFU Cuenca, lifting sin cirugía Cuenca | High intent |
| /es/servicios/manchas | tratamiento de manchas Cuenca | High intent |
| /es/servicios/drenaje-postoperatorio | drenaje linfático postoperatorio Cuenca | High intent, low competition |
| /es/servicios/eliminacion-lunares | eliminación de lunares con láser Cuenca | High intent |
| /es/servicios/exosomas | exosomas Cuenca, rejuvenecimiento facial Cuenca | Mid intent |
| /es/servicios/masajes-reductores | masajes reductores Cuenca | Mid intent |

**Each page should contain:**
1. An H1 with the treatment plus location (for example "Depilación Láser en Cuenca").
2. 600 to 1000 words of original copy: what it is, benefits, who it is for, what to expect, sessions, aftercare.
3. A short FAQ section (3 to 5 questions) targeting long-tail queries.
4. A clear booking call to action (WhatsApp and the contact form) above and below the fold.
5. Unique meta title and meta description with the keyword and "Cuenca, Ecuador."
6. Mirror each page in English under /en for the expat audience.

**Effort:** 2 to 4 hours per page. Stagger over the month, starting with the top 5.

**Done when:** Each priority treatment has its own URL, indexed in GSC, with unique copy and meta tags.

### Task 2.2 — Add LocalBusiness structured data (schema.org)

**What:** Add JSON-LD structured data marking the site as a LocalBusiness (or MedicalBusiness / DaySpa).

**Why it matters:** Schema tells Google exactly where the business is, its hours, services, and contact details. It reinforces the "Cuenca, Ecuador" signal and helps win rich results and the local pack. It directly counters the Cuenca Spain versus Cuenca Ecuador confusion.

**Steps:**
1. Add a JSON-LD block to the site (in the Next.js layout or per page) with: name, address (with addressCountry EC), geo coordinates, telephone, openingHours, priceRange, url, and sameAs links to the real Instagram and Facebook profiles.
2. Add the geo coordinates for Edificio Plaza Médica.
3. Include an areaServed of Cuenca, Azuay, Ecuador.
4. Validate with Google's Rich Results Test.

**Effort:** 2 to 3 hours.

**Done when:** The schema validates with no errors in Google's Rich Results Test and appears on the homepage and contact page.

---

### Task 2.3 — Verify hreflang between Spanish and English

**What:** Confirm the es and en versions are correctly linked with hreflang tags.

**Why it matters:** hreflang tells Google to serve Spanish to Spanish searchers and English to English searchers (the expat audience). Done wrong, the two versions can compete with each other or the wrong one ranks.

**Steps:**
1. Check that each page declares hreflang="es-EC" pointing to the /es version and hreflang="en" to the /en version, plus an x-default.
2. Confirm the tags are reciprocal (each page points to its counterpart and to itself).
3. Validate with a hreflang checker tool.

**Effort:** 1 to 2 hours.

**Done when:** hreflang tags are reciprocal, valid, and present on all paired pages.

---

### Task 2.4 — Reinforce local geographic signals across the site

**What:** Make sure every page leaves no doubt the business is in Cuenca, Ecuador, not Cuenca, Spain.

**Why it matters:** The biggest organic obstacle is that Spain's Cuenca dominates generic search results. Every local signal you add tilts Google toward Ecuador.

**Steps:**
1. Use "Cuenca, Ecuador" or "Cuenca, Azuay" in footers, titles, and key headings rather than "Cuenca" alone.
2. Keep the NAP (Name, Address, Phone) identical everywhere it appears, matching the Google Business Profile exactly.
3. Embed a Google Map of the location on the contact page.
4. Reference local landmarks or neighborhoods where natural (for example the Plaza Médica building, the area of the city).

**Effort:** 1 to 2 hours.

**Done when:** NAP is consistent site-wide, "Cuenca, Ecuador" appears in titles and footer, and a map is embedded on /contacto.

## Phase 3 — Ongoing (compounding over 3 to 6 months)

### Task 3.1 — Publish 2 to 4 blog articles per month

**What:** Grow the Journal beyond the current 3 articles with consistent, useful, treatment-related content.

**Why it matters:** Informational content brings traffic, builds topical authority, and earns the trust Google needs before it ranks the commercial service pages. It is also where the brand demonstrates expertise.

**Article backlog (priority order):**
1. ¿Qué son los exosomas y para qué sirven en la piel?
2. HIFU: cuántas sesiones necesito y cuánto duran los resultados.
3. Depilación láser: cuántas sesiones y cuidados antes y después.
4. Drenaje linfático después de una cirugía: guía completa de recuperación.
5. Cómo eliminar manchas en la cara: opciones y qué funciona.
6. Skin boosters versus cremas: qué hidrata de verdad.
7. Cuidados post-operatorios: errores comunes que retrasan la recuperación.
8. PDRN de salmón: qué es y por qué es tendencia.

**Steps per article:**
1. Target one informational keyword and write 800 to 1500 words.
2. Use a clear H1, subheadings, and a short FAQ.
3. Link internally to the relevant treatment page (this passes authority to the pages that convert).
4. Add a soft call to action to book an evaluation.
5. Mirror the best performers in English for the expat audience.

**Effort:** 2 to 3 hours per article.

**Done when:** A content calendar exists and at least 2 new articles publish each month, each linking to a service page.

## What NOT to do

- **Do not chase national or international rankings** against large players like Dra. Aura Ruiz (Colombia) or equipment vendors. They rank for treatment terms but never take your local clients. You would burn effort for traffic that does not book.
- **Do not buy backlinks** or use spammy link directories. On a new domain this can trigger distrust and set you back months.
- **Do not expect results in weeks.** Local SEO on a fresh domain is a 3 to 6 month build. The Google Business Profile and reviews work is the exception and can show movement in 30 to 60 days.
- **Do not keep treatments as anchor sections only.** Split them into real pages (Task 2.1). This is the difference between ranking for one keyword and ranking for eight.
- **Do not let NAP drift.** Different addresses or phone formats across the web confuse Google and weaken local trust.

## Competitor reference

**Tier 1 — Local Cuenca, Ecuador (your real client competition)**
- BIOLASER (biolaserecuador.com): strongest local website, dermatology and laser focused. The one to study and outpace on medical-grade treatment pages.
- SKY Centro Estético, Ámbar Centro Estético & Spa, Zafiro Depilación: Facebook-only, no website. Your modern site is already an advantage over them.
- FotoDepil, Definitive: national franchises strong on laser hair removal and brand recognition.

**Key insight:** Almost no local competitor has a real, content-rich website. Jenny Vera Spa already does. Feed it content and local signals and that becomes a durable edge.

**Tier 2 — Content and term competitors (national/international)**
- Dra. Aura Ruiz (Bogotá, Colombia), Vien Estética (Quito), equipment vendors. They rank for treatment terms but do not compete for local clients. Win the local-intent versions of their keywords, do not fight them nationally.

## Measurement

Review monthly in Google Search Console and Google Analytics 4:
- Impressions and clicks for "[treatment] Cuenca" queries.
- Indexed page count (should grow as you add treatment pages and articles).
- Google Business Profile: views, calls, direction requests, and review count.
- Bookings or WhatsApp inquiries attributed to organic and the profile.

Set a simple baseline now, then compare at 30, 60, 90, and 180 days.
